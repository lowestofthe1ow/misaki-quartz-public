Informal definition of a limit §

Let f(x) be defined on an open interval containing a, except possibly at a itself. If f(x) gets arbitrarily close to L for all x sufficiently close to a, we say that f(x) approaches the limit L as x approaches a, and we write:

x→alim​f(x)=L

A function may fail to have a limit at a point in its domain.

Formal (ϵ-δ) definition of a limit §

Let f(x) be a function defined at every number in some open interval containing a, except possibly at the number a itself.

x→alim​f(x)=L

—if and only if…

Given any ϵ>0, however small, there exists a δ>0 such that:
0<∣x−a∣<δ→∣f(x)−L∣<ϵ

Show that limx→3​f(x)=1 given f(x)=2x−5 §

We must show that:

0<∣x−3∣<δ→∣(2x−5)−1∣<ϵ

Or, equivalently:

0<∣x−3∣<δ→2∣x−3∣<ϵ≡0<∣x−3∣<δ→∣x−3∣<2ϵ​​

Given δ=2ϵ​≡2δ=ϵ:

0<∣x−3∣<δ→2∣x−3∣<2δ≡0<∣x−3∣<δ→2∣x−3∣<22ϵ​≡0<∣x−3∣<δ→2∣x−3∣<ϵ∴x→3lim​f(x)=1​

Show that limx→2​f(x)=4 given f(x)=x2 §

We must show that:

0<∣x−2∣<δ→∣x2−4∣<ϵ

Or, equivalently:

0<∣x−2∣<δ→∣x+2∣∣x−2∣<ϵ
